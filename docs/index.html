Which Roblox spikes actually stick?

Every studio knows the feeling: concurrent users jump 30% overnight. The question that matters is, will it hold? I built a system that watches CCU across 1,000 Roblox games, matches each spike to recent creator (YouTube) coverage, and tracks what happens next. This is what 689 backtested episodes say.

The event study

Both cohorts spike to 1.45 multiplier baseline at detection with near-identical pre-trends. Then the creator-linked cohort holds a persistent gap. The weekly ripple in both curves is weekend seasonality and co-moves and the vertical gap between the lines is the effect. Shaded bands are bootstrap 95% CIs.

Methodology & honest limits

  • Matched controls: each creator-linked spike is paired to a control spike matched on baseline CCU and timing (7 days). 657 usable pairs.
  • Out-of-sample: the detection ruleset predates the Feb–Jun replay window, so the bulk of episodes are a true out-of-sample test. Code/script/exploit spam videos excluded (16% of the pool).
  • Correlational, not causal: creators may select games already poised to hold. Matching covers size, timing, and spike magnitude, not everything a creator can see.
  • Bias direction is known: controls are "no detected coverage" against 151 tracked channels, so some had coverage I can't see, which biases the measured gap downward. The true effect is likely larger.
  • Documented null: an earlier, smaller live-only sample (64 pairs) showed no significant effect. Underpowered and contaminated by a Friday-scheduling artifact. Both results are reported; the larger supersedes the smaller for an identifiable reason.
